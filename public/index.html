About

Traffic Accidents in California

Studying and analyzing data on traffic collisions is crucial to understanding the causes of accidents and preventing them from happening in the future. Additionally, for legal departments and insurance agencies involved, it is important to investigate data on accidents to determine the costs and effectiveness of laws and regulations in place.

Objective

Preliminary Analysis

Conduct basic analysis on accident data, such as trends over time with respect to differences between geographical regions

Accident Predictor

Build and select the best model to predict the number of accidents in a district on a day to help respective authority plan resources

Predict Accident Severity

Build and select the best model to predict accident severity based on factors determined from Preliminary Analysis

Analysis

Time Series Analysis
Compare accident count/injured/death by different cities
Cagetory Basic Analysis
Basic Analysis on Pie, Stack and Percent Stack Chart
Category Info over Region
Overall analysis based on various categories by regions
Category Info over Region and Time
Time-based analysis with various categories info
Collision Count Forecasting
Forecasting traffic collisions count for California's most populated counties
Collision Severity Classification
Random Forest Classifier to predict level of serverity of accidents
